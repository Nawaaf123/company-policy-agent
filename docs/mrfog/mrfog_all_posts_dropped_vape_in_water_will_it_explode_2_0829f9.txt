Dropped Vape in Water: Will It Explode? What You Should Do Next
Quick Answer
If you dropped your vape in water, the good news is:
Most vapes will not immediately explode after getting wet.
However, that does not mean they’re safe to use.
Water can damage the battery, short-circuit internal electronics, and create conditions that increase the risk of overheating, malfunction, or battery failure later.
The safest approach is:
Stop using the device immediately
Remove power if possible
Dry it thoroughly
Inspect it carefully before attempting to use it again
A vape that has been submerged in water may appear to work normally while still having hidden internal damage.
First Things First: Don’t Panic
Almost every long-term vaper has had a moment like this.
Maybe your vape slipped into:
a sink
a toilet
a swimming pool
a drink
a puddle
the washing machine
The first reaction is usually panic.
Many people immediately ask:
“My vape fell in water. Is it going to explode?”
In most situations:
No, not immediately.
But water exposure should always be treated seriously.
Why Water and Vapes Don’t Mix
Modern vape devices contain several components that dislike moisture.
These include:
lithium-ion batteries
circuit boards
charging ports
sensors
heating coils
When water enters these areas, it can create electrical pathways where they shouldn’t exist.
This can lead to:
short circuits
corrosion
battery damage
inconsistent performance
The longer water remains inside the device, the greater the risk becomes.
Can a Vape Battery Explode After Getting Wet?
This is the concern most people have.
The answer is:
It’s possible, but uncommon.
Lithium-ion batteries can become dangerous if they are:
punctured
crushed
overcharged
internally short-circuited
Water can contribute to internal shorts.
If the battery has been compromised, you may experience:
overheating
swelling
unusual heat
strange smells
battery failure
In rare cases, damaged lithium-ion batteries can enter a condition known as:
Thermal Runaway
This occurs when the battery generates heat faster than it can dissipate it.
Although rare, thermal runaway can result in:
smoke
fire
battery rupture
This is why experts recommend not using a vape immediately after water exposure.
What Happens When a Vape Gets Wet?
The effects depend on:
How Much Water Entered
A splash is very different from full submersion.
How Long It Was Underwater
A device recovered within seconds may survive with minimal damage.
A vape left underwater for several minutes faces a much higher risk.
Whether It Was Powered On
A powered-on device is more vulnerable to immediate short circuits.
Device Design
Some devices offer limited water resistance.
Most disposable vapes do not.
Signs Your Water-Damaged Vape May Be Unsafe
Before attempting to use the device again, check for warning signs.
Excessive Heat
If the vape becomes unusually warm while idle:
Stop using it immediately.
Swollen Battery
Battery swelling indicates internal damage.
Do not charge or use the device.
Strange Smells
A chemical odor can indicate battery failure.
Auto-Firing
If the vape activates on its own:
Dispose of it safely.
Charging Problems
If charging becomes inconsistent after water exposure, internal damage may have occurred.
What To Do Immediately After Dropping a Vape in Water
Step 1: Remove It Immediately
The less time underwater, the better.
Every second matters.
Step 2: Turn It Off
If possible, power the device down immediately.
Step 3: Remove Components
If your device allows it:
remove the pod
remove the tank
remove the battery
This helps prevent further damage.
Step 4: Dry the Exterior
Use:
paper towels
microfiber cloths
Avoid applying excessive pressure.
Step 5: Air Dry
Leave the device in a dry location.
Many experts recommend waiting:
24–48 hours
before testing it.
Should You Put a Vape in Rice?
Many people still use this method.
The truth is:
Rice is not the best option.
Rice may absorb some moisture, but it often leaves:
dust
starch particles
inside charging ports and openings.
Silica gel packs are generally more effective.
Can Disposable Vapes Survive Water Damage?
Sometimes.
Disposable vapes contain:
batteries
electronics
just like rechargeable devices.
Because they cannot be easily disassembled, drying them thoroughly can be more difficult.
If a disposable vape becomes submerged:
The safest option is often replacement.
What About Pod Systems and Rechargeable Devices?
Rechargeable devices may have a better chance of recovery because:
pods can be removed
batteries may be removable
components can dry separately
However, internal damage can still occur.
How Long Should You Wait Before Using a Wet Vape?
A common mistake is testing the device too soon.
Even if it appears dry externally, moisture may remain inside.
A general recommendation is:
Minimum: 24 hours
Better: 48 hours
Best: Until you’re confident it’s completely dry
Patience can prevent additional damage.
Can Water Damage Affect Flavor?
Yes.
Even if the device survives, water may affect:
coil performance
wicking material
vapor production
flavor consistency
A coil exposed to water often produces weaker flavor afterward.
Preventing Water Damage in the Future
Accidents happen, but several habits can reduce risk.
Use Protective Cases
Especially while traveling.
Keep Devices Away From Drinks
Many water incidents happen on desks and tables.
Avoid Bathroom Counters
Sinks are surprisingly common vape hazards.
Use Water-Resistant Devices When Available
Although no vape is truly waterproof, some devices provide better protection than others.
Can a Water-Damaged Vape Ever Be 100% Safe Again?
Not always.
This is important.
A vape may:
turn on
produce vapor
charge normally
while still having unseen battery damage.
This is why any device showing unusual behavior after water exposure should be replaced.
The cost of a new device is usually far less than the consequences of a battery failure.
Final Thoughts
So, if you dropped your vape in water, will it explode?
In most cases:
No.
But water exposure should never be ignored.
A water-damaged vape can develop hidden problems that may appear hours or even days later.
stop using it immediately
dry it thoroughly
inspect it carefully
replace it if there is any sign of battery damage
When it comes to lithium-ion batteries, caution is always the smarter choice.
Will a vape explode immediately after falling in water?
Usually not. Most vapes will not explode immediately after water exposure, but hidden battery damage can create safety risks later.
Can I charge my vape after it gets wet?
Not until it is completely dry. Charging a wet vape can increase the risk of short circuits and battery damage.
How long should I wait before using a vape that got wet?
Most experts recommend waiting at least 24 to 48 hours and ensuring the device is completely dry before testing it.
Is a disposable vape safe after falling in water?
Sometimes, but disposable vapes are difficult to inspect internally. If the device was fully submerged, replacement is often the safest option.
What are signs of battery damage after water exposure?
Common warning signs include overheating, swelling, charging issues, strange smells, and automatic firing.
Does putting a vape in rice work?
Rice may absorb some moisture, but silica gel packs are generally more effective and less likely to leave debris inside the device.
Can water permanently damage a vape?
Yes. Water can corrode internal components, damage batteries, and reduce overall performance even if the device still appears functional.
Should I use a vape if it still works after getting wet?
Only after it has been thoroughly dried and inspected. If you notice any unusual behavior, discontinue use and replace the device.
